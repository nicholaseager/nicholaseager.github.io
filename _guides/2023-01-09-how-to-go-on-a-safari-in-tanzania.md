## The Animals

There are numerous different species of animals that can be found in the Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, Tarangire, Lake Manyara, and Lake Eyasi, among other parks and lakes. Some of the animals that you might see in each of these parks and lakes are listed below:

| Park | Animals |
|----------------|
| Serengeti | Lions, leopards, elephants, buffaloes, rhinos, giraffes, zebras, antelopes, hyenas, jackals, wildebeest, gazelles, warthogs, baboons, and a wide variety of bird species. |
| Ngorongoro Crater | Lions, leopards, buffaloes, giraffes, elephants, rhinos, antelopes, hyenas, jackals, gazelles, warthogs, baboons, flamingoes, and a wide variety of bird species. |
| Tarangire | Elephants, giraffes, zebras, lions, leopards, buffaloes, rhinos, antelopes, hyenas, jackals, wildebeest, gazelles, warthogs, baboons, and a wide variety of bird species. |
| Lake Manyara | Flamingos, elephants, giraffes, baboons, antelopes, lions, leopards, buffaloes, gazelles, warthogs, and a wide variety of bird species. |
| Lake Eyasi | Flamingos, baboons, antelopes, zebras, gazelles, warthogs, and a wide variety of bird species. The lake is also home to the Hadzabe and Datoga tribes, who have lived in the area for centuries. |

Overall, there are many different species of animals living in these parks and lakes, so there is a good chance that you will see a wide variety of them while you are there.

## Best Time to Go

The best time to visit Tanzania depends on your interests and what you hope to see and do while you are there.

Depending on the season, Tanzania's weather can change dramatically. Since the foliage is less dense and the weather is dry and bright during the dry season, which lasts from June to October, it is typically thought to be the best time to travel there. This makes it easier to spot wildlife. Although the weather might be more erratic and the roads can be trickier to maneuver, the wet season, which lasts from November to May, is still an excellent time to visit.

The annual wildebeest migration, which occurs between June and October, is what makes the Serengeti famous. Huge herds of zebras and wildebeest are also roaming the park at this time in search of new grass. It is best to go during the dry season if you want to see this spectacle. Although the wildebeest migration is the Serengeti's most well-known spectacle, there is a wide variety of other wildlife to observe all year long. A variety of animals, including large predators like lions and leopards as well as herbivores like buffalo and giraffe, can be seen at Ngorongoro Crater.

## Getting There

There are several ways to get to and around Tanzania, depending on your starting location and your preferred mode of transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

#### Flying to Tanzania
The most convenient way to get to Tanzania is to fly. Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) is located about an hour's drive from the park. From the airport, you can either rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to your final destination.

#### Driving to the Parks
You can drive to many parks in Tanzania from a variety of locations if you prefer to travel by car. It takes about 6-7 hours to drive from Arusha, which is a popular starting point for many tourists. The drive is beautiful and provides a chance to pause and visit other sights along the way.

#### Taking a Bus
Another option is to take a bus from a variety of locations in Tanzania. There are several companies that offer bus services to the parks, and the trip takes about 8-9 hours from Arusha.

## Getting Around

Going with an organized tour definitely has its perks. But if you're more independent, you can definitely rent a car and go on safari by yourself. However, it is a good idea to research your options and to compare prices and reviews before making a decision. Research car rental companies, check the rental requirements, choose a car that is suitable for the conditions (i.e. a 4x4 for off-roading and rough terrain), and reserve the car in advance if you plan on going during peak season. Definitely do your own due-diligence and research so that you can be safe, and find something for your own needs and budget.
